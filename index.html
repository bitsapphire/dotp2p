About

.p2p is the first of several namespaces that are managed by BlockchainDNS (BDNS), an opt-in alternative to traditional DNS. The traditional domain name system is plagued with problems like domain name seizures, man-in-the-middle attacks, and broken name ownership incentives. BDNS solves these problems using transparent public ledger technology comparable to what is used in Bitcoin.

The design of BDNS is guided by the perspective that blockchain-based systems with tokens (like Bitcoin or Namecoin) are best viewed as Decentralized Autonomous Companies, not currencies. This means that BDNS can properly incentivize growth, capture income, and scale to manage the thousands of transactions per second necessary to support ICANN-scale namespaces.

Key Features

Resistance to seizures

Domains cannot be seized because they are not controlled by any single entity. You cannot coerce anyone into erasing a record from a cryptographically secured transparent public ledger.

Resistance to spying

Since only the owner of a domain can modify the corresponding BDNS record, you can trust that a self-signed certificate is associated with a given name.
